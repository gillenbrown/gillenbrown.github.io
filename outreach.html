Outreach and Advocacy

Michigan Dark Skies

I'm a member of the Michigan Dark Skies group [link], which raises public awareness of light pollution and advocates for dark sky friendly lighting at the local level.

I have been involved with several projects with Michigan Dark Skies. I've met with the DEVELOPERS of an upcoming DEVELOPMENT in Ann Arbor to encourage lighting that reduces light pollution. While the project is still in the early stages, the DEVELOPERS have committed to use dark sky friendly light fixtures and light bulbs.

I've also met with the ANN ARBOR CITY COUNCIL STUDENT ADVISORY COUNCIL to discuss light pollution issues. This group is a branch of city council composed of students that [what do they do?]. Ann Arbor City Council is considering adding streetlights in areas with a lot of student housing. I worked with this group to conduct a lighting audit of these areas, and ensure that any additional streetlights added cause as little light pollution as possible.

Lastly, I've engaged in outreach to educate the public about light pollution and how to stop it. I gave a talk at Ignite Ann Arbor, an event consisting of 5 minute talks with 20 slides that automatically advance every 15 seconds. I gave a quick overview of what causes light pollution, how we can reduce it, and why we should care about it.

Outreach

In March, I will give a talk at Ann Arbor Nerd Nite about the chemical evolution of the universe. I'll walk through all the basic nucleosynthetic processes and what objects these processes operate in. This will let me walk through the periodic table and discuss where all the elements we know so well came from. This is a great story that doesn't get told much in science communication, and I think it deserves to be told more.
